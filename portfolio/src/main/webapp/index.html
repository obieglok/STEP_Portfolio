More About me

I am an incoming 3rd year student of Computer Science and Business in Trinity College Dublin. Ever since I stepped foot in Trinity, I knew it was the place where I wanted to study. Through series of ups and downs I have managed to secure a spot in this university. As a First Generation college student, I am extremely proud of this accomplishment.

You may wonder and ask but what made you choose your course? Well let me tell you!

Ever since I was young I used to sneak into my aunts room to turn on the computer and play around on it. Then when I was 7, I have received my first ever laptop and I believed that I would become a hacker. Unfortunately I haev alwasy found it hard to get started to learn html, java etc. I would have started a course but never finished it. Then in Transition Year I singed up to take part in Bridge 21 in Trinity. It was a week long programme in which you would get a chance to learn some coding. And the spark has been ignited ever since. From participating in many different weeks with Bridge 21, I was assured that CS what exactly the thing that I wanted to do. But, there was a problem called Physics . See, throughout school I loved Science, especially Biology and Chemistry, but I never took interest in physics. Upon reading all Computer Science courses I found out that physics plays an important role in them, and I just couldn't make myself sign up to do it. Upon further research I stumbled across CSB- Computer Science and Business course and I knew it was the right fit, and here I am!

Throughout the past two years in college I have been actively involved in mentoring through Student to Student, Bridge 21 and TAP. Some of my tasks as a mentor include: going out to schools to give presentations, campus tours and helping TY students with coding. Why do I do it? Well I like to give back to my community and inspire other girls to join STEM. When I was younger I enjoyed hearing about college and peoples' experiences with it, and they inspired and motivated me. So now I would like to be that motivation for someone.

This year I will be on Coder Dojo's Commitee looking after their Social Media! So stay tuned!

Hand Gesture Recogniton Tool

As part of my Software Enginering Module, my team mates and myself have created a Hang Gesture Recognition Tool. This tool allows you to use your computer mouse through a series of gestures. This limits the need to touch computer equipment which will lead to a decrease in cross-contamination risks. We were requested by a company called SureWash to complete this project and we hope it will get some good use!

Some things I learned through it:

  • Your Client may not know what they are actually looking for.
  • I realised command line isn't that bad!
  • I learned more about OpenCV and Python
